💡 Did you know?

The ship inside the Shipwreck Museum sailed the Mediterranean around 300 BC and sank just off Kyrenia — it's the oldest Greek merchant ship ever recovered. A local diver found it by accident in 1965, and it took archaeologists years to raise and piece the hull back together.

Kyrenia Harbour

Start with the most recognizable view in Kyrenia. Enjoy the sea breeze, moored boats, and relaxed waterfront cafes.

📍 Open in Google Maps

Kyrenia's horseshoe-shaped harbour dates back to the Venetian period and remains the most photographed spot in town, ringed by old stone warehouses that have been converted into restaurants and cafés.

🚶‍♂️ 5 mins walk (400m) along the pier

Kyrenia Castle

Move from the harbour into the massive stone fort walls that form the historical core of Kyrenia.

📍 Open in Google Maps

The Byzantines built the first fortress here in the 7th century to guard against Arab raids. It was captured by Richard the Lionheart in 1191, expanded by the Lusignans, and took its current form under Venetian rule around 1489.

🚶‍♂️ 2 mins walk inside the castle walls

Shipwreck Museum

Located directly inside the castle grounds, this museum showcases one of the oldest recovered merchant shipwrecks in existence.

📍 Open in Google Maps

The ship on display sank around 300 BC and is the oldest Greek merchant vessel ever recovered — found by a local diver in 1965 and painstakingly reassembled piece by piece after a multi-year excavation.

🚶‍♂️ 10 mins walk (800m) into the narrow backstreets

Old Town Streets

Wind through narrow stone-paved lanes directly behind the harbor line, packed with historic local character.

📍 Open in Google Maps

These lanes sit just behind the harbour front and retain much of Kyrenia's pre-tourism character, with narrow stone alleys that are far quieter than the waterfront just a few steps away.

🚶‍♂️ 5 mins walk (300m) uphill
📸 Best Photo Spot

Girne Castle Sea View Lookouts

Climb up onto the expansive defensive walls to capture stunning ocean vistas. This panoramic viewpoint provides historical context.

📍 Open in Google Maps

The castle's sea view terraces provide visitors with stunning panoramic views of Kyrenia Harbour, making them one of the best locations for photography and sightseeing.

🚶‍♂️ 10 mins walk back to the waterfront

Harbour Sunset Finish

Return to the harbor stone wall edge for sunset. It makes an ideal endpoint for seaside dining and evening photos.

📍 Open in Google Maps

As the sun drops, the harbour stones and old warehouse façades pick up a warm golden light — locals consider this the best hour of the day to be here, whether for photos or just a slow walk.

Practical info

  • This route is easy on foot once you are in central Kyrenia.
  • Parking can be busy, so arrive before sunset time.
  • Keep the castle/museum stop earlier to avoid closing-time problems.

Insider tip

If you have extra time after visiting the castle, walk beyond the main harbour cafés into the smaller side streets. They're often quieter and offer local shops and hidden viewpoints away from the busiest tourist areas.
