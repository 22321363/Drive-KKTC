Before you go.

Our specific route pages cover local stops. This page handles the structural baseline utilities—driving conditions, local banking systems, borders, and gear setups.

Getting around

A rental car is highly recommended—several deep valley tracks and cape horizons across these networks have zero public transit access.

Driving Rules

  • Traffic strictly travels on the left hand side of the lane (matching UK layouts). Steering wheels sit on the right.
  • Speed ceilings stay at 50 km/h inside urban environments; rural mountain and peninsula tracks grow exceptionally narrow and winding.
  • Seatbelts are mandatory for all passengers across every row.
  • There are absolutely no tolls or transit access tariffs applied across TRNC road networks.
  • Fuel networks are highly concentrated near major hubs but become scarce deep in Karpaz or Güzelyurt boundaries. Fill up early.

Rental Cars & Border Lines

Northern territory rentals cannot cross past Green Line checkpoints into Southern Cyprus due to distinct mandatory insurance policy requirements. Plan checkpoint crossings as pedestrian walks, or organize a separate rental split across lines.

Money & Currency

  • The baseline operating currency is the Turkish Lira (TRY). Euros and British Pounds are taken inside main tourist nodes, but paying Lira saves on exchange premiums.
  • Card transactions function seamlessly across urban hotels, main markets, and upscale diners. Rural stops remain strictly cash-dependent.
  • Local automated ATMs are highly common within town hubs.

Staying Connected

  • Picking up an eSIM or a localized data SIM module on arrival is recommended for seamless navigation routing.
  • Standard EU roaming packages typically do not extend coverage limits to TRNC transmitters. Double-check tracking rates.
  • Wi-Fi networks are standard across capital cafes, dropping off slightly in deep regional outposts.
