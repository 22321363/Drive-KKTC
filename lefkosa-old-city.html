💡 Did you know?

Büyük Han was built in 1572 — just one year after the Ottomans took Nicosia from the Venetians — and was modelled on Koza Han in Bursa, Turkey. Selimiye Mosque, meanwhile, started life in the 13th century as the Gothic Cathedral of St. Sophia, and only became a mosque after the Ottoman conquest in 1571.

🏛️

Kyrenia Gate

Start at one of the prime portals into the walled city, serving as a clean architectural introductory point.

📍 Open in Google Maps

Kyrenia Gate was built by the Venetians in 1562 as part of Nicosia's star-shaped fortifications, originally named Porta del Proveditore. It's one of only three surviving gates in the old city walls, alongside Famagusta Gate and Paphos Gate, and for centuries it was the main road connecting the capital to the port town of Kyrenia.

🚶‍♂️ 10 mins walk (700m) down Girne Caddesi
🏛️

Sarayönü / Atatürk Square

A central urban meeting point where traditional buildings, municipal offices, and local cafes align.

📍 Open in Google Maps

This square sits roughly where the Lusignan and later Ottoman "Saray" (governor's palace) once stood — hence the name Sarayönü, meaning "in front of the palace." Today it's anchored by a large Atatürk monument and functions as the informal heart of North Nicosia's daily life.

🚶‍♂️ 4 mins walk (300m) through pedestrian zones
🏛️ 📸 Best Photo Spot

Büyük Han

One of the strongest cultural anchors on the island. This restored Ottoman inn features courtyard artisan craft shops.

📍 Open in Google Maps

Büyük Han was built in 1572 under the island's first Ottoman governor and modelled on Koza Han in Bursa. It later served as Nicosia's central prison under British rule before being restored in the 1990s into the gallery and craft-shop space it is today.

🚶‍♂️ 3 mins walk (200m) winding east
🏛️

Selimiye Mosque Area

A deep landmark featuring prominent architectural layers, tracking shifts through multiple eras of local history.

📍 Open in Google Maps

Originally the Gothic Cathedral of St. Sophia, this building was consecrated in the 13th century and remains one of the best-preserved examples of Lusignan-era Gothic architecture in the Eastern Mediterranean. Two minarets were added after it was converted into a mosque in 1571.

🚶‍♂️ 2 mins walk (100m) to the covered market
🏛️

Bandabuliya Market

A covered produce and textile hub, giving visitors direct access to the classic market transactions of Nicosia.

📍 Open in Google Maps

Bandabuliya has served as Nicosia's covered municipal market since the Ottoman period, and it's still where many locals buy produce, cheese and fresh bread rather than tourist souvenirs — a genuinely working market, not a display for visitors.

🚶‍♂️ 5 mins walk (400m) back towards the checkpoint
🏛️

Arasta Street

End with a vibrant walking commercial lane full of souvenir collections, coffee lounges, and everyday street life.

📍 Open in Google Maps

This street sits just behind Selimiye and Büyük Han and has long served as the commercial spine of the old city, lined with small workshops, tailors and coffee houses that have operated for generations.

Practical info

  • Comfortable shoes are enough; the route is mostly walkable.
  • Avoid the hottest part of the day in summer.
  • Keep small cash for cafés, snacks or small shops.

Insider tip

Visit Büyük Han in the late afternoon, around an hour before sunset. The courtyard becomes quieter, the stone architecture glows with warm light, and you'll find the best atmosphere for photography or enjoying a traditional Turkish coffee.
